## 1. Global Market Overview

### 1.1 Market Size & Growth

| Metric | 2024 | 2025 | 2026 (est.) | CAGR 2024–2028 |
|--------|------|------|-------------|----------------|
| Global fitness equipment market | $14.2B | $15.1B | $16.0B | 6.2% |
| Commercial gym equipment segment | $8.5B | $9.1B | $9.7B | 6.8% |
| Strength equipment sub-segment | $3.8B | $4.1B | $4.4B | 7.1% |
| Cardio equipment sub-segment | $4.2B | $4.5B | $4.8B | 5.9% |
| Free weights sub-segment | $1.2B | $1.3B | $1.4B | 6.5% |

### 1.2 Key Market Trends

1. **Boutique fitness boom**: Small-format gyms (200–500 m²) driving demand for compact multi-stations and space-efficient equipment
2. **Premiumization**: Gym owners upgrading to commercial-grade equipment to reduce maintenance and improve member retention
3. **Functional training shift**: Growing demand for cable machines, functional trainers, and free weight areas over fixed-path machines
4. **Recovery & wellness**: Rowing machines and low-impact cardio gaining share
5. **Retrofit market**: Existing gyms replacing aging equipment (5–7 year replacement cycle for commercial use)
6. **Home-to-commercial crossover**: High-end home gym users purchasing commercial-grade equipment
7. **Green manufacturing**: Buyers increasingly asking about sustainable materials, recyclable packaging, carbon footprint

### 1.3 Supply Chain Dynamics

- China produces ~65% of world's commercial gym equipment
- Shandong province is China's largest gym equipment manufacturing cluster (30%+ of national output)
- Raw material costs (steel) fluctuate with global commodity markets; Q235 steel price range: $500–650/ton
- Shipping rates from Qingdao: relatively stable post-pandemic; 40HQ to US West Coast ~$2,500–3,500; to Europe ~$3,000–4,000
- Anti-dumping: No current anti-dumping duties on gym equipment from China to major markets

## 3. Competitive Landscape Summary

### 3.1 Global Tier Structure

| Tier | Brands | Price Level | Typical Buyer |
|------|--------|------------|---------------|
| Tier 1 (Premium) | Technogym, Life Fitness, Hammer Strength, Cybex | $$$$ | Premium health clubs, 5-star hotels |
| Tier 2 (Mid-Premium) | Precor, Matrix, Star Trac | $$$ | Mid-to-upscale gyms, YMCAs |
| Tier 3 (Mid-Range) | BYFIT target zone, Force USA, Body-Solid | $$ | Independent gyms, boutique studios, budget chains |
| Tier 4 (Economy) | Generic Chinese brands, no-name OEM | $ | Home users, very budget gyms |

### 3.2 BYFIT Competitive Position

- **Sweet spot**: Tier 3 with Tier 2 quality at Tier 3 pricing
- **Key advantage**: Commercial-grade specs (3 mm steel, 45 kg/m³ foam) at prices typically associated with lighter-gauge products
- **Weakness**: Brand recognition in Western markets is low; no local service network
- **Mitigation**: Build brand through distributor partnerships; offer comprehensive video installation guides and remote technical support
